Executive Summary
Modernizing VA.gov is a mission-critical effort that requires continuity of service, enterprise-grade security, and a seamless, Veteran-centric digital experience - all delivered within a highly regulated federal environment. Success depends not only on new front-end capabilities, but on a resilient data and operational foundation that can integrate legacy and modern systems while enabling continuous improvement at scale.
Splunk serves as that foundation. Already operating as a core component of VA's enterprise digital ecosystem through the Enterprise Logging Warehouse, Splunk enables VA.gov modernization with reduced risk by leveraging existing, proven capabilities across security monitoring, observability, analytics, and governance. Splunk provides full-stack visibility across identity, applications, infrastructure, and user journeys - allowing VA to maintain uninterrupted service during transition while improving performance, reliability, and trust.
By embedding security, compliance, and real-time operational insight into the fabric of VA.gov, Splunk enables VA to deliver a secure, resilient, and continuously improving digital experience for Veterans, their families, and stakeholders.

VA's intent to replace VA.gov represents a transformation of the nation's primary digital entry point for Veteran benefits, healthcare, and services. As described in this RFI, success requires more than a new front-end platform. The replacement must maintain uninterrupted service for millions of users, integrate a

3. Splunk's Approach: A Data Foundation for VA.gov Modernization
Splunk enables VA.gov modernization by serving as the data foundation and operational nervous system across the digital ecosystem. Rather than introducing a parallel or duplicative platform, Splunk builds on its existing VA footprint to reduce risk, preserve continuity, and accelerate coordination across teams during platform replacement.
Today, Veterans face a fragmented experience, with critical information scattered across paper records, disparate hospital systems, and multiple benefits databases. The Splunk Digital Command Center brings all these digital signals together--creating a unified, real-time view of every Veteran's care journey.
With Splunk, the VA gains the ability to proactively monitor and manage essential systems. The platform can instantly identify when a component of the VA.gov site is at risk of downtime, when a veteran's claim encounters a processing bottleneck, or when sensitive health data faces unauthorized access. By translating complex data into actionable insights, Splunk ensures that the systems supporting Veterans are fast, secure, and reliable.

The modernization of the VA.gov website is a critical initiative to enhance the digital experience for clinicians, staff, and veterans. By integrating Splunk and AppDynamics, VA can deliver a seamless, easy-touse site that proactively ensures exceptional performance and reliability. Key benefits of this approach include:
Unified Full-Stack Observability: Combining Splunk's comprehensive observability platform with AppDynamics' application performance monitoring provides complete visibility across the entire digital ecosystem--from network and infrastructure to applications and user experience. This unified approach enables VA to detect and resolve issues faster, minimizing downtime and disruptions for all users.
Proactive Monitoring and Real-Time Insights: Splunk's real-time streaming analytics and AppDynamics' detailed transaction tracing allow VA to monitor every interaction on VA.gov without sampling, ensuring 100% visibility into performance. This empowers the VA's OIT teams to identify and address potential problems before they impact clinicians, staff, or veterans.
Enhanced User Experience for Clinicians and Veterans: With digital experience monitoring capabilities, VA can optimize the site's responsiveness and usability, delivering a reliable and intuitive platform for virtual visits, claims processing, and accessing benefits. Operational Efficiency and Collaboration: The integrated solution breaks down silos between application, infrastructure, and security teams, reducing tool sprawl and enabling streamlined workflows. AI-powered analytics and predictive alerting accelerate root cause analysis and incident resolution, freeing resources to focus on mission-critical tasks.
Security and Compliance: Leveraging AppDynamics and Splunk's combined security and observability capabilities ensures that VA.gov remains resilient against cyber threats while maintaining compliance with federal regulations.

Splunk strengthens VA.gov security by providing real-time monitoring, anomaly detection, and fraud identification across identity proofing, authentication, and access workflows. These capabilities support next-generation digital identity initiatives while minimizing friction for Veterans. Splunk also automates the collection and reporting of FISMA and FedRAMP audit evidence, embedding compliance into day-today operations and reducing manual reporting burden.
At scale, Splunk's observability platform applies AI-driven analytics and dynamic baselining to continuously learn normal behavior across VA.gov services and Veteran usage patterns. This intelligence

3.3 Veteran-Centric Digital Experience
Splunk enables continuous improvement of the Veteran experience through real-user monitoring (RUM), user journey analytics, and performance visibility across web and mobile channels. By analyzing sign-in, recovery, navigation, and benefit workflows, VA can identify friction points, improve page responsiveness, and maintain consistent service quality across benefit portfolios--supporting measurable improvements in reliability and usability without disrupting access.
Splunk delivers continuity of service during VA.gov modernization by providing full-stack observability across front-end Veteran experiences and back-end mission systems, spanning legacy platforms and modern cloud-native services. Through the AppDynamics Observability suite, VA gains real-time visibility into how Veterans experience critical digital journeys - such as sign-in, benefits access, healthcare scheduling, and claims workflows - while simultaneously monitoring the downstream application services, APIs, databases, and infrastructure that support those interactions.
AppDynamics enables VA to correlate user experience metrics (latency, errors, abandonment) with application and service performance, allowing teams to rapidly identify whether issues originate in the browser, mobile experience, application logic, third-party dependencies, or underlying infrastructure. This front-to-back correlation is especially critical during platform transitions, where parallel operation of legacy and replacement components can introduce hidden dependencies and failure points.

7. Known Risks, Limitations, and Tradeoffs
VA.gov replacement presents inherent risks that must be actively managed during modernization:
· Transition Complexity: Parallel operation of legacy and replacement systems increases complexity. Mitigation: Unified observability across environments preserves visibility and control.
Page 7

· Data Volume and Signal Management: High telemetry volume can introduce noise. Mitigation: Filtering, prioritization, and analytics focus attention on mission-relevant signals.
· Change Velocity: Rapid updates may introduce instability. Mitigation: Continuous baselining and anomaly detection support controlled change.
· Toolchain Fragmentation: Over-specialization can create visibility gaps. Mitigation: Splunk serves as an integration layer rather than a point solution.
These tradeoffs are manageable when addressed proactively through governance and observability.
8. Phased Approach to VA.gov Replacement
Splunk supports a phased modernization approach that reduces risk while enabling incremental improvement:
Phase 1 - Baseline & Visibility
Establish end-to-end observability across current VA.gov and dependent systems; baseline performance and experience metrics.
Phase 2 - Parallel Operations
Monitor legacy and replacement components simultaneously to identify dependencies and risks early.
Phase 3 - Incremental Transition
Support gradual traffic shifts and feature rollouts, validating stability and experience before expansion.
Phase 4 - Optimization & Continuous Improvement
Use analytics and user journey insights to refine performance, usability, and scalability over time.
9. Measuring Success for a Modernized VA.gov
While specific targets are determined by VA, Splunk enables objective evaluation of modernization success through metrics such as:
· Service availability and reliability across critical journeys · Authentication success and recovery completion rates · Page load times and transaction latency · Reduction in unplanned outages or degraded experiences · Improved cross-team situational awareness and response times
These indicators help VA assess whether VA.gov is delivering a secure, resilient, and Veteran-centric digital experience.
