1. Service Records and Honors Page

Figure 1: Service Records and Honors Page
Description: This interface presents the "Your records and honors" page within the authenticated VA.gov experience, providing access to official service history, medals and distinctions, medical records, and status verification letters. It also supports document retrieval actions such as requesting DD-214 records.
What this demonstrates:
 Centralized access to authoritative Veteran records  Secure handling of sensitive personal and service data  Self-service document discovery and retrieval  Integration across multiple record-owning systems
Why it matters to the replacement model: This figure highlights the need for a replacement architecture that preserves data integrity, provenance, and controlled access while unifying records from multiple legacy sources into a single Veteran-facing experience.

6. Secure Sign-In Page

Figure 6: Secure Sign-In Page
Description: This interface shows the Secure Sign In page, allowing Veterans to authenticate using approved identity providers such as Login.gov or ID.me.
What this demonstrates:
 Federated identity integration  Tiered identity assurance  Separation of authentication from downstream services  Enforcement of secure access controls
Why it matters to the replacement model: Any replacement approach must align with Zero Trust principles, supporting strong identity verification while decoupling identity from application logic.
